## Core Trip Context

- Travel dates are fixed: **June 12 to July 12, 2026**.
- There are **30 nights** and no extra nights can be added because of the
  plane tickets.
- The trip is nature-first and travels north to south.
- There will be **no rental car**. Plans must work using trains, buses,
  ferries, flights, and walking.
- The travelers enjoy scenic trains and want to experience landscapes from
  trains, but do not want to spend excessive time in northern/eastern Hokkaido
  merely to ride trains.
- Prefer worthwhile stops that fit naturally along transfer routes rather
  than large detours.

## Current Route Decisions

- Keep the current overnight structure. Do not add an extra northern or
  eastern Hokkaido night.
- Do not remove another destination solely for a Furano/Biei train ride.
- The Furano/Biei Norokko loop remains an optional train-focused Sapporo day,

- Prioritize the existing Resort Shirakami journey from Aomori to Akita as the
  main scenic-train experience.

## Top Three Proposed Route Upgrades

These are high-value transfer-day stopovers. They are recommendations, not yet
confirmed bookings:

1. **Onuma Park: Lake Toya to Hakodate**
   - Stop at Onuma-Koen Station for lake and island-bridge walking trails.
   - Continue to Hakodate afterward.
   - Adds Hokkaido nature without traveling in the wrong direction.

2. **Kakunodate: Akita to Tokyo**
   - Leave luggage at the station and spend roughly 3-4 hours in the samurai
     district.
   - Continue directly to Tokyo on a later Komachi Shinkansen.
   - Uses part of a Tokyo arrival day; Tokyo currently has four nights.

3. **Himeji: Kyoto to Hiroshima**
   - Visit Himeji Castle and Koko-en Garden before continuing to Hiroshima.
   - This requires an early Kyoto departure and careful planning around the
     Hiroshima/Miyajima schedule.

Runner-up: stop at Kami-Suwa between Tokyo and Matsumoto for Lake Suwa, the
station footbath, and sake breweries.

- Sapporo to Noboribetsu/Lake Toya: Upopoy, Noboribetsu Hell Valley, Oyunuma,
  natural footbath, Lake Toya lakeside, and evening fireworks if operating.
- Lake Toya to Hakodate: Onuma Park.
- Aomori to Akita: enjoy Resort Shirakami itself; Juniko/Aoike and Furofushi
  Onsen require more time and careful schedules.
- Akita to Tokyo: Kakunodate; Morioka is another possible stop.
- Tokyo to Matsumoto: Lake Suwa/Kami-Suwa.
- Matsumoto to Kanazawa: properly pace the Alpine Route rather than adding
  unrelated stops.
- Kanazawa to Kyoto: Kaga Onsen, Fukui, or Tsuruga if time permits.
- Kyoto to Hiroshima: Himeji first; Kurashiki or Onomichi are alternatives.
- Hiroshima to Yakushima: keep this mainly as a direct transfer day.
