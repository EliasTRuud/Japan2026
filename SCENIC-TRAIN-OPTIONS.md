# Scenic Train Options

This note records the discussion about adding a short Hokkaido scenic-train
experience without spending much additional time in northern Japan.

## Current Recommendation

Do not remove another destination or add an overnight for Furano/Biei.

The trip already includes stronger and more efficient scenic rail experiences:

- Sapporo to Noboribetsu, Lake Toya, and Hakodate: Pacific coast, volcanic
  landscapes, Funka Bay, and Mount Komagatake.
- Hakodate to Aomori: Hokkaido Shinkansen.
- Aomori to Akita aboard Resort Shirakami: the trip's main scenic train,
  following the Sea of Japan coast.

If an extra short train ride is wanted from Sapporo, Otaru is the easiest
choice. Trains are frequent, the line includes an attractive coastal section,
and the return time is flexible.

## Optional Furano/Biei Scenic Loop

This is an optional train-focused loop from Sapporo, not a recommended change
to the main route:

```text
Sapporo
  -> Furano Lavender Express
Furano
  -> Furano-Biei Norokko scenic train
Biei
  -> local Furano Line train
Asahikawa
  -> Lilac/Kamui limited express
Sapporo
```

The compact version is approximately:

```text
07:41  Sapporo -> Furano
09:44  Arrive Furano
11:53  Furano -> Biei on the Norokko
12:51  Arrive Biei
13:11  Biei -> Asahikawa
13:43  Arrive Asahikawa
~14:00 Asahikawa -> Sapporo
~15:30 Arrive Sapporo
```

Times are planning examples only. Recheck the operating calendar, timetable,
connections, and seat availability before using this route.

### Why It Is Optional

- The genuinely scenic Norokko section is only about one hour.
- Sparse connections make the loop consume most of a day.
- Mid-June is early for Furano's famous lavender fields.
- Biei's best-known countryside locations are awkward without a car.
- Biei/Furano is north and east of Sapporo, while the next main stop is south
  at Noboribetsu and Lake Toya. It is not an efficient onward route.

### When It Is Worth Doing

Take the loop only if riding the sightseeing trains is itself the priority.
Otherwise, keep the existing route and spend the time in Sapporo, Otaru, or
on an earlier southbound departure.
